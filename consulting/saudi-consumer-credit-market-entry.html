The brief: is there a venture here — and how would you build it?

An anonymised GCC investor asked a simple question: does Saudi Arabia’s consumer-credit market have room for a new entrant, and if so, what should it look like? The slides below are the working deck — market sizing, a competitive read, a five-pillar strategy, an alternative-data underwriting engine, the operating stack, and five-year unit economics.

Client identity is withheld and the deck has been anonymised. Figures are built on public GASTAT and SAMA data plus modelled estimates, and are illustrative of the analytical approach.

Saudi Arabia is 35 million people — and half the workforce earns under SAR 5,000.

Three independent lenses point to a widening retail credit gap.

Twenty-seven million working-age adults, sized by age and income.

Payday and microfinance are the best risk-adjusted way in.

Incumbents are structurally locked out of the expat and informal segments.

Under 10% of the market is served — this is nascent, not competitive.

The vision: a payday-to-microfinance lender for the underserved.

Five pillars, each built on what incumbents cannot do.

Payday first to build the data factory, then microfinance.

Behavioural data compresses losses — the economics work at 4–4.5× leverage.

The edge is an alternative-data underwriting engine.

Amount, tenor and price all flex by risk bin and cycle.

The operating stack: originate, manage, collect — agentic and LLM-native.

Six functions, built lean around the model.

A pricing map across every product and ticket band.
