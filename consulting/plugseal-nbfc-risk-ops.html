Data security & compliance

Organized around three questions any NBFC will ask first: where does our data live, does anyone else ever touch it, and is every vendor in the chain actually compliant. Items marked open are genuine decisions still to be made — not gaps we're hiding.

1. Data residency & on-premise processing

Where case data, call recordings, extracted documents, and model logs are stored and processed — including whether any component routes data through infrastructure or model APIs outside India, or whether processing can run fully on the NBFC's own premises / VPC.

Mitigation — open

Not yet decided — depends on final infra architecture (on-prem / VPC vs. hosted).

2. No cross-client data use

Whether data or outputs from one NBFC client are ever used, directly or indirectly — including model fine-tuning or prompt calibration — to benefit another client.

Mitigation

Per-client logical (and where required, physical) data isolation. No cross-client model training or fine-tuning on any client's case data, ever.

3. Vendor / sub-processor DPDP & PII compliance

Any third-party service Plugseal itself depends on — OCR providers, LLM APIs, cloud hosting — becomes a sub-processor under DPDP and a reportable subcontractor under RBI's Outsourcing Directions. Each must independently meet DPDP safeguards and PII handling standards, not just Plugseal itself.

Mitigation — open

Sub-processor list and their individual DPDP/PII compliance status to be finalized and disclosed.

4. RBI vendor due diligence & audit rights

RBI's 2025 Outsourcing Directions treat any data-handling vendor as a "Service Provider," requiring documented due diligence and contractual rights for the NBFC, RBI, and auditors to inspect Plugseal's systems directly.

Mitigation

Due-diligence pack (security architecture, data flow diagram, sub-processor list) prepared ahead of onboarding. Inspection rights for the NBFC, RBI, and auditors accepted contractually upfront.

5. DPDP Data Processor obligations

The NBFC is the Data Fiduciary; Plugseal is a Data Processor bound by a Data Processing Agreement covering security safeguards and purpose limitation — Plugseal cannot use data beyond the contracted purpose.

Mitigation

Standard DPA template ready ahead of client conversations, not drafted case-by-case under deal pressure. Purpose limitation committed contractually.

6. Model auditability & AI governance (RBI FREE-AI framework)

RBI's FREE-AI report extends board accountability to third-party AI vendors — the NBFC's board remains accountable for what Plugseal's agents do, and expects the same rigor validating a vendor's models as its own.

Mitigation

Every agent decision traceable to the specific rule or model/prompt version that produced it. Human sign-off gates at every stage already built into the architecture (see Full Architecture) — not added on request.

7. Algorithmic bias / fair-lending exposure

A credit or fraud agent that treats demographic patterns (age, family structure) as a proxy risk signal — rather than a specific financial inconsistency — is exactly the embedded bias RBI's FREE-AI framework and DPDP's algorithm-screening provisions target.

Mitigation

Every flag must be grounded in a stated financial fact (e.g., "co-applicant has no independent income for this loan size"), never a demographic pattern alone. Enforced at the prompt/schema level, not left to reviewer judgment.

8. Breach notification timing

A single incident can trigger three overlapping notification clocks — CERT-In (6 hours), RBI's cybersecurity framework, and DPDP's Data Protection Board — each with different timelines and recipients.

Mitigation

Incident response built around the tightest window — CERT-In's 6 hours — as the binding commitment in the vendor agreement, satisfying the other two by design.

9. Retention vs. deletion conflicts

DPDP's purpose-limitation principle expects deletion once processing ends; RBI/KYC/PMLA rules require retention (commonly 7–10 years) for the same data. Getting this wrong in either direction is a violation.

Mitigation

Plugseal's systems never auto-delete on a fixed timer — retention decisions defer to the NBFC's own regulatory basis, not an independent policy of ours.

10. Vendor personnel due diligence

RBI's Outsourcing Directions require documented background-check evidence for any vendor staff with data access, plus tracking of "essential personnel" with backup arrangements.

Mitigation — open

Background-check process and essential-personnel documentation to be defined.

11. Subcontractor transparency & certifications

RBI requires that a vendor not subcontract without the NBFC's prior approval, and remains liable for subcontractors' conduct — including whatever model API providers Plugseal itself depends on.

Mitigation — open

Subcontractor map and any security certifications (ISO 27001 / SOC 2 status) to be confirmed and disclosed.
