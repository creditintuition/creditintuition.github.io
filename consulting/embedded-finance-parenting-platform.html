the platform Fintech
Payments
Closed Loop Wallet Live
What: the platform Cash wallet — users load cash and spend only on Platform Store. Exempt from RBI PPI licensing as a closed-loop instrument.

Current mechanics: Load ₹2,000 get ₹2,100 (5% bonus). Earn the platform Cash via surveys, social follows, spin-to-win. Redeem against store purchases.

Strategic value: Trains financial behaviour on platform. Every load is a micro-transaction. Builds the usage history that makes future credit underwriting possible.

Limitation: No interchange revenue. No float income at current scale. Pure loyalty mechanic until semi-closed wallet is added.

Next step: Track load frequency and redemption rate per cohort. Users who load 3+ times are the highest-intent segment for credit and insurance cross-sell.
Semi-Closed Wallet Future
What: Wallet spendable at third-party hospital and pharmacy POS — not just Platform Store. Load ₹5,000, get ₹250 bonus (5% discount) redeemable at network providers.

Why compelling: Maternity and infant healthcare spend is ₹60K–2L over the first year. If the platform can capture even 20% of that as wallet load, float income and interchange become meaningful.

Regulatory reality: Semi-closed PPI requires RBI PPI licence — or a licensed partner (Paytm, PhonePe, slice). the platform should partner, not apply. Licence takes 2–3 years and ₹5Cr net worth minimum.

Partner model: White-label a licensed PPI issuer. the platform controls the UX, partner holds the licence. Revenue share on interchange (1–1.5% of spends) + float income on loaded balances.

Prerequisite: Build hospital network first (needed for packages anyway). Semi-closed wallet extends the same network to payment acceptance.
Credit
Childbirth Loan Near-term
What: ₹1–3L loan disbursed at week 27. 3 pre-EMIs before delivery, then 18–24 month repayment. Highest revenue product.

Why the platform wins: Due date is known. Originate at week 24 before hospital booking. No bank has this signal.

Model: CLM2 — NBFC funds 100%, the platform provides 8–10% FLDG, earns 5–6% spread.

Revenue/customer: ₹18,000–22,000 (spread + insurance commission + processing fee).

Next step: Identify NBFC partner. Draft CLM2 term sheet. Build credit policy with CIBIL 680+ floor.
BNPL / Checkout Live
What: ₹500–5,000 BNPL on Platform Store. Diapers, formula, baby gear. Repeat category, 2–3x/month frequency.

Model: LSP — NBFC pre-approves limit at onboarding. Zero marginal underwriting per transaction.

IRR: 24–36% on small ticket. Low default severity. Good unit economics at scale.

Extensions: Pharmacy credit; doctor consultation credit; lab test credit — all on the same revolving limit.

Next step: Pilot on top 20 Platform Store SKUs. Measure D30 default. Scale if <3%.
IVF / Fertility Loan Near-term
What: ₹1.5–3L per cycle, structured for 2 cycles. Direct disbursement to clinic.

Why the platform wins: TTC >6 month users search IVF content explicitly. No insurance exists. Loan is the only product.

Risk: 30–40% success rate. Mitigate via direct clinic disbursal + 15% down payment.

Extensions: Egg freezing loan; fertility medication credit disbursed to pharmacy.

Next step: Partner with Indira IVF, Nova IVF, Cloudnine. Direct settlement API.
School Fee Loan Near-term
What: ₹50K–3L/yr upfront to school, parent repays in EMIs. Zero-interest via school subvention (2–3%).

Why the platform wins: Tracks child age — prompts school fee loan 6 months before admission season. Structural timing edge.

Model: B2B2C. School pays subvention. NBFC funds. the platform earns distribution fee.

Extensions: Preschool loan; coaching loan; back-to-school device loan.

Next step: Shortlist 50 CBSE schools in top 10 cities for pilot.
Car Loan Referral Future
What: Lead-gen for car loans at the "new family car" moment — parents with 0–12 month newborns upgrade vehicles at high rates.

Model: Pure DSA referral. HDFC Bank, ICICI Bank, Mahindra Finance pay ₹3,000–8,000 per disbursed loan. Zero capital from the platform.

Next step: Push notification campaign to 0–6 month cohort. Measure conversion before formalising DSA agreement.
Insurance
Complication Cover Near-term
What: NICU, maternal ICU, emergency C-section complications — bundled on top of the childbirth loan. Not sold standalone.

Why it works: Genuinely uncertain, high severity. Claim rate 8–12%. Real insurance economics unlike maternity.

Pricing: ₹1,500–2,500 premium in EMI. the platform earns 15–18% commission as corporate agent.

Next step: Approach Niva Bupa / Star Health for group "obstetric complication" product — sidesteps maternity waiting period entirely.
Parent Term Life Near-term
What: Credit-linked term life. Outstanding loan repaid by insurer if primary earner dies during tenure.

Why now: New parent moment = highest life insurance intent. No waiting period on term life.

Economics: ₹500–2,000/yr premium. 25–35% first-year commission. 5–7.5% renewal from year 2.

Next step: HDFC Life / ICICI Pru have pre-built credit-life APIs. One-tap add-on at loan checkout.
Child Health Floater Live (pilot)
What: Family floater adding newborn from Day 1. Offered to 0–6 month cohort.

Economics: ₹8,000–15,000/yr. 17–20% first-year commission. 80%+ renewal rate.

Extensions: OPD-only paediatric plan; vaccination package insurance.

Regulatory path: IRDAI Corporate Agent — ₹50L net worth, up to 9 insurer tie-ups.
Personal Accident Future
What: PA cover for primary earner. ₹2,000–5,000/yr, ₹10–50L sum assured.

Model: Bundle into the platform Family Plan subscription. Adds value, marginal claim risk, 15–20% commission.

Extensions: Two-wheeler accident cover (Tier 2 commuters); hospitalisation daily cash.
the platform Family Plan Future
What: ₹199–499/month subscription bundling wallet bonus, child health insurance, discounts, emergency credit line.

Revenue stack: ₹2,400–6,000/yr fee + insurance commission + incremental credit origination.

Risk: Churn kills the model. Start at ₹199/month, prove retention before scaling price.

Extensions: Telemedicine access; discount pharmacy network; priority SLA.
Investments
Delivery Savings SIP Near-term
What: Goal-based SIP — "₹50,000 delivery fund ready by Week 38." 3–6 month horizon. Target: TTC and 1st trimester.

Model: MFD — NISM Series V-A + ARN. Zero capital. Trail commission 0.5–1% AUM.

Timing to launch: 4 months from starting NISM process.

Extensions: Emergency fund SIP; vaccination corpus SIP (₹300/month, ₹25K over 3 years).
Child Education Corpus Near-term
What: Long-horizon SIP — "IIT Fund / IIM Fund." ₹2,000–10,000/month, 10–18 year horizon. Target: 0–6 month motherhood cohort.

Economics: ₹5K SIP × 1% trail × 10yr = ₹600 cumulative/user. 100K SIP users = ₹60Cr AUM at year 5.

Extensions: Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana facilitation (girl child, 8.2% govt-backed); PPF-linked education plan.

Next step: NISM V-A first. AMC partner: Mirae, Axis, or DSP.
Digital Gold Future
What: "Buy ₹100 gold for baby's first month." Milestone-linked accumulation with physical redemption at 5 years.

Model: Partner SafeGold / Augmont / MMTC-PAMP. 0.5–1% spread or ₹29/month platform fee.

Cultural fit: Gold gifting at birth (mundan, naming ceremony) is deeply Indian. Digital wrapper on an existing behaviour.

Extensions: Sovereign Gold Bond distribution (1% commission, tax-efficient).
Wealth Mgmt Referral Future
What: HNI users (HHI ₹30L+) referred to SEBI-registered RIA or wealth platform. ₹3,000–10,000 per opened account.

Partners: Scripbox, Fisdom, INDmoney, 1Finance.

Risk: Cannot give investment advice without RIA licence. Keep it referral-only, not advisory.

Platform Pay — Insurance strategy
Why insurance does not work standalone — childbirth
What insurance is designed for
low
Low frequency
Event happens rarely — death, critical illness, accident. Pooling across millions makes pricing work.
high
High severity
When it does happen, the cost is catastrophic. Insurance transfers that tail risk to the pool.
?
Uncertain timing
Neither insurer nor customer knows when the event will occur. That uncertainty is the product.
Why maternity fails all three tests
95%
Near-certain event
If a woman is pregnant, delivery will happen. Claim probability is ~95%. No pooling benefit.
low
Moderate severity
Normal delivery ₹60K–1.5L. Painful but not catastrophic. Insurance sub-limits reflect this.
9mo
Timing is known
Due date is predictable to within 2 weeks. No uncertainty. Insurer cannot price this as risk.

The insurer's dilemma: If no waiting period exists, every pregnant woman buys the policy, claims immediately, and cancels. The insurer loses money on every policy. Waiting periods are the only structural defence — which means maternity insurance is useless for the customer who needs it most: the woman who is already pregnant. The only exception is complication cover — NICU, maternal ICU, emergency C-section complications. These are genuinely uncertain, genuinely severe, and genuinely insurable. That is the only insurance product that makes sense bundled with the platform's childbirth loan.
Platform Pay — Insurance strategy
Why insurance does not work standalone — IVF & pregnancy
the platform's data advantage is also its insurance problem. The platform knows about pregnancy and IVF precisely because users are already in those journeys. That certainty — which makes the platform valuable for lending — makes insurance structurally impossible for the same events.
Pregnancy — 44% of the platform's base
Largest single segment on the platform
1
User arrives already pregnant
the platform's peak engagement is weeks 8–36. By the time she downloads the app and tracks her due date — she is pregnant. The event is not in the future. It is happening now.
2
Every insurer excludes existing pregnancy
HDFC ERGO explicitly states: "Pregnant women cannot buy maternity insurance." Niva Bupa, Care Health, Star Health all enforce the same. IRDAI permits this exclusion.
3
Waiting period outlasts the pregnancy
Minimum waiting period is 9 months (Niva Bupa Aspire). A woman in her 1st trimester who buys today cannot claim for this delivery. The policy covers her next pregnancy — if she has one.
Verdict for the platform's core user
Maternity insurance is not available to 44% of the platform. The product does not exist for them at the moment of highest intent.
IVF / Fertility — TTC >6 months segment
High ticket, high intent, deeply underserved
1
User signals IVF intent explicitly on platform
the platform tracks TTC users who have been trying for 6+ months. Searches for IVF clinics, hormone protocols, and fertility specialists are explicit intent signals. IVF is planned — not uncertain.
2
IVF insurance does not exist in India
No standalone IVF insurance product exists from any Indian insurer. A handful of premium group plans mention IVF as a sub-benefit — at sub-limits of ₹50,000–1,00,000 on a ₹2–3L procedure. Effectively uninsured.
3
Why — same actuarial problem as maternity
Once a couple decides on IVF the procedure will happen. Cost is certain (₹1.5–3L/cycle). Outcome is uncertain (30–40% success rate) — but the expense is not. You cannot insure a planned expenditure.
Verdict for the platform's TTC user
IVF insurance is structurally non-existent. The need is ₹3–8L per patient. The only product that works is a loan.

The best insurance sub-limit available is ₹2L — but only after a 2-year waiting period, only for the procedure (not medications which cost ₹60–80K/cycle), and only at approved clinics. Scenario A — has employer cover
She has group health insurance from her employer. Maternity cover likely included with zero waiting period. OPD visits, scans, and delivery are partially covered.
the platform insurance value-add
Zero. She is already covered. Selling her another maternity policy is mis-selling.

Scenario B — no insurance, already pregnant
She has no health cover. She is already in her 1st or 2nd trimester. Every insurer will reject her maternity application or impose a waiting period that outlasts her current pregnancy.
the platform insurance value-add
Zero for this pregnancy. She cannot procure meaningful cover. The product simply does not exist for her current need.
Insurance — structurally unavailable Credit — only viable product
Scenario C — OPD & doctor visits
OB consultations and scans are already happening. Standard health plans either cover OPD (employer group) or don't (most individual retail plans). the platform cannot retroactively insure visits that have already occurred or are in progress.
the platform insurance value-add
Zero for ongoing OPD. Insurance is either already covering it or will never cover it. A healthcare credit line fills the gap for visits not covered.
Insurance — already decided Credit — fills uncovered OPD costs
The conclusion across all three scenarios
the platform's data advantage — knowing the due date, tracking the pregnancy week — arrives too late to originate insurance. The window to sell maternity insurance closed before the user downloaded the app. Credit is the only product the platform can originate at the moment of highest intent.

Platform Pay — Insurance strategy
Insurance works — but only as a bundled product
Two insurance layers make the loan complete. Neither is standalone — both are bundled onto the childbirth loan. One protects the borrower from catastrophic cost overrun. The other protects the family (and the platform's book) if either parent dies or is disabled.

Three risk layers — Customer, Hospital, Transaction. Hospital payment holdback is a proposal for founder discussion.
Category Sub-category Risk Mitigation
Customer Credit risk Borrower defaults — income disruption, over-leverage, willingness-to-pay drop post-birth ML credit scoring. CIBIL 680+ floor. FOIR check. NACH mandate. Soft in-app collections before hard recovery
Fraud risk Synthetic identity, income inflation, straw borrower Aadhaar OTP auth. Video KYC above ₹1.5L. Welcome call post-disbursal. Dedupe across applications
Hospital Overbilling Hospital inflates bill to extract higher loan disbursement Empanelled hospitals only with published tariffs. RCU cross-checks every invoice. Variance triggers escalation
Zero DP risk Hospital waives 20% customer contribution to boost conversion DP collected by the platform directly. Hospital receives only 80% regardless. Contractually prohibited
Service quality Substandard care — borrower stops repaying citing deficiency NABH accreditation + 4.0+ Google rating at onboarding. Quarterly monitoring. Proposal: 5–10% holdback released on loan completion
Transaction Complication C-section, NICU, maternal ICU — cost overrun increases default probability Bundled NICU + maternal ICU cover rolled into EMI. Insurer pays overrun directly to hospital
Service level Poor experience creates collections friction even without genuine default intent SLA in hospital agreement. Dedicated the platform ops support. Dispute ≠ EMI suspension

Insurer P&L benchmarks — Life & General/Health (FY2024)
Why this matters for the platform: Insurance economics look attractive on headline PAT — but PAT in life insurance is distorted by policyholder vs. shareholder account separation, reserve releases, and investment income. The normalized view (% of premium) reveals the true operating engine. For a corporate agent earning 15–25% commission, the question is: how much of that premium dollar actually flows through to profit for the underwriter — and thus how stable is the commission pool?

Life insurance reads
• PAT of 2–3% on GWP looks modest but VNB margins of 24–28% capture the true new-business economics — driven by long-duration product spreads, not underwriting
• Investment income (~17–20% of GWP) is the silent engine — life insurer profitability is structurally investment-dependent
• Commission at 8–10% of GWP is the corporate agent's addressable income pool — stable and contractual
• LIC's 8.6% PAT/GWP is an outlier driven by back-book reserve releases, not replicable economics
General / health insurance reads
• Combined ratios above 100% (Star, ICICI Lombard, Niva Bupa, New India) mean underwriting loses money — profit is entirely investment income
• Commission at 17–22% of net premium is higher than life — but the underwriter margin is razor thin or negative
• Niva Bupa's 1.7% PAT/net premium at 108% combined ratio signals a growth-phase company subsidising CAC — not a stable P&L
• Implication for the platform: health commission economics look better, but the underwriters the platform partners with are under structural margin pressure — commission pools can be cut if claims spike
